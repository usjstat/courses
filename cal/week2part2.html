1.5 Conditional Probability

Conditional probability is a way to measure the likelihood of an event when we already know that another event has happened.

Definition

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be two events, with \(\Pr(B) \ne 0\).

The conditional probability of \(A\) given \(B\) is:

\[ \Pr(A \mid B) = \frac{\Pr(A \cap B)}{\Pr(B)} \]

This formula tells us how to compute the probability of \(A\) under the condition that \(B\) has already occurred.

Why is it Important?

In real-world situations, we often have partial information, and we want to update our probabilities based on that.

Examples for CAL

  • Given that a stock has increased for three consecutive days (event \(B\)), what is the probability that it will increase again tomorrow (event \(A\))?
  • Given that a client is from a premium category (event \(B\)), what is the probability that they prefer a high-risk investment (event \(A\))?
  • Given that a transaction occurred outside business hours (event \(B\)), what is the probability it gets flagged for review (event \(A\))?

These are all conditional probability questions.

Interpretation

  • If \(\Pr(A \mid B)\) is close to 1, then \(A\) is very likely to occur when \(B\) occurs.
  • If \(\Pr(A \mid B)\) is close to 0, then \(A\) is unlikely given \(B\).
  • If \(\Pr(A \mid B) = \Pr(A)\), then events \(A\) and \(B\) are independent.

Important Notes

  • Conditional probability changes our perspective - we now focus only on outcomes where \(B\) has occurred.
  • The denominator \(\Pr(B)\) must be greater than zero for the formula to make sense.

Activity: Call Centre Probability

A CAL call centre observes the following data over a month:

  • 65% of all calls are from existing clients (Event \(B\)).
  • 30% of all calls are escalated (the process of transferring a customer’s call from a frontline agent to a higher-level support representative) to supervisors (Event \(A\)).
  • 20% of all calls are from existing clients and are escalated (Event \(A \cap B\)).
  1. What is the probability that a call is escalated given that it is from an existing client?
  2. What is the probability that a call is from an existing client given that it is escalated?
Solution

Let’s write the known values:

  • \(\Pr(B) = 0.65\)
  • \(\Pr(A) = 0.30\)
  • \(\Pr(A \cap B) = 0.20\)

1. Probability that a call is escalated given it is from an existing client:

\[ \Pr(A \mid B) = \frac{\Pr(A \cap B)}{\Pr(B)} = \frac{0.20}{0.65} \approx 0.3077 \]

So, about 30.8% of existing client calls are escalated.

2. Probability that a call is from an existing client given it is escalated:

\[ \Pr(B \mid A) = \frac{\Pr(A \cap B)}{\Pr(A)} = \frac{0.20}{0.30} \approx 0.6667 \]

So, about 66.7% of escalated calls are from existing clients.

Activity: Meeting Scheduling

Let:

  • \(A\) = first appointment is with an investment advisor
  • \(B\) = second appointment is with a compliance advisor
  1. Find \(\Pr(B \mid A)\)
  2. Find \(\Pr(B \mid A')\)
Solution

Total = 5 advisors

  1. If first is investment ??? 2 investment, 2 compliance left
    \[ \Pr(B \mid A) = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5 \]

  2. If first is compliance ??? 3 investment, 1 compliance left
    \[ \Pr(B \mid A') = \frac{1}{4} = 0.25 \]

Definition: Independent Events

Two events \(A\) and \(B\) are independent if:

\[ \Pr(A \mid B) = \Pr(A) \quad \text{or} \quad \Pr(A \cap B) = \Pr(A) \cdot \Pr(B) \]

Mutually Exclusive vs. Independent Events

Mutually Exclusive

  • \(\Pr(A \cap B) = 0\)
  • \(\Pr(A \cup B) = \Pr(A) + \Pr(B)\)

Example: A transaction is either flagged for review or cleared - not both.

Independent

  • \(\Pr(A \cap B) = \Pr(A) \cdot \Pr(B)\)
  • \(\Pr(A \mid B) = \Pr(A)\)

Example: Receiving a portfolio update email and a phone call from your advisor are independent.

Note: Mutually exclusive events with nonzero probabilities are not independent.

Summary Table

Concept Notation/Condition Interpretation
Mutually Exclusive \(\Pr(A \cap B) = 0\) Cannot happen together
Independent Events \(\Pr(A \cap B) = \Pr(A)\Pr(B)\) One does not affect the other
